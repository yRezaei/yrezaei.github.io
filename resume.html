Publications

Arm Prosthesis Simulation On A VR L-shaped Workbench Display System Using A Neuro-headset
10th ICDVRAT, Gothenburg, Sweden. (Jan 2016)

This paper outlines a collaborative project between the Bonn-Rhein-Sieg University of Applied Sciences (Germany) and the Federal University of Uberlândia (Brazil) to develop a virtual training simulator that accelerates and improves upper limb prosthesis control. While this paper focuses on the technical system design, future work will involve creating learning tutorials and conducting a comprehensive evaluation.

User Interface Design for Disabled People Under the Influence of Time Efficiency and Costs
HCI International Conference on Human-Computer Interaction. (June 2014)

This publication addresses common challenges in designing applications for disabled users—specifically narrow target groups, specialized user interfaces, and interdependence issues—while considering time, efficiency, and cost constraints. It proposes a new perspective on User-Centered Design (UCD), dividing and analyzing the UI architecture across three spaces (User, Need, and Application) and offers an algorithmic guideline to reduce interdependence between interaction modalities.

Robust EEG Time Series Transient Detection With A Momentary Frequency Estimator For The Indication of An Emotional Change
35th German Conference on Artificial Intelligence. (Sep 2012)

This paper presents an adaptive time frequency analysis approach for EEG signals, examining both the theoretical framework and practical applications. A momentary frequency estimation algorithm is introduced and tested on EEG data from concentration experiments, where emotional changes can cause transient signals masked by noise and artifacts. Findings show that the estimator detects these transients accurately and robustly, even under challenging conditions.
