Who I Am

Hey there! I’m Yashar—software engineer by profession, builder by nature, and someone who believes that true joy comes from sharing life with the people you love.

A Hands-On Creator

I’ve always been drawn to figuring out how things work. Whether I’m designing and building my own kitchen cabinets or repairing a broken appliance, there’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of watching something I crafted (or fixed) come to life. I’ll happily lose myself in a DIY project, diving into videos and research about tools, materials, and the science behind them.

Work, Dreams & Ambitions

Professionally, I’m a senior software engineer who thrives on solving complex problems and creating innovative solutions. I’ve worked across various industries—robotics, automotive, distributed systems—and love the challenge of diving into new projects that expand my skill set. Looking ahead, I’m aiming to grow into a solutions architect or engineering leader, steering projects from concept to reality. I’m also keen on exploring new ideas that can make a meaningful impact, continuously learning and adapting to keep pace with the ever-evolving tech world.

My Day-to-Day

I’m an early bird, up around 7 a.m. every morning (though bedtime tends to fall closer to midnight). Each day starts with coffee, feeding my cat, and a quick check of my little garden. I like to keep my schedule semi-flexible, juggling work, family time, and personal projects. After a long day, I’ll often unwind with a hot cup of tea—then I’m back at it, chipping away at whatever goal I’m currently chasing.

The People Who Matter

I’m fortunate to share life’s ups and downs with my wonderful wife, Mozhgan. She’s the best part of my day, and our relationship keeps me focused on what truly matters: loyalty, love, and mutual support. I’m also someone who values friendships deeply. I enjoy the camaraderie of the office, diving into small talk, and hearing about people’s own journeys. Even after moving abroad and building a new life in Germany, I keep finding ways to connect, laugh, and create new memories with friends—old and new.

Staying True to My Roots

I was born in Karaj, near Tehran, but my family hails from Tabriz, a city known for its hardworking and business-minded culture. Growing up there taught me that real progress comes from dedication and discipline—nobody hands you anything on a silver platter. I still cherish the traditions of visiting elders during the Iranian New Year and gathering with relatives to celebrate cultural events.

Life Philosophy & Values

I believe that what fuels success is determination. Nothing in life is free, and while knowledge might be at our fingertips, applying it takes serious effort. I’m all for lending a hand—whether it’s helping a friend move forward in a meaningful way or guiding someone to solve a tough problem. But I also think money alone doesn’t solve anything in the long run; it’s about mindset, resilience, and taking ownership of your path.

What Drives Me

At the heart of everything I do is my dedication. No matter what I face—a complex coding challenge, a demanding DIY project, or simply the ups and downs of life—I rely on resilience and discipline. I keep my eyes on the finish line and push forward, fueled by the satisfaction of seeing a project completed or an idea brought to life.

Fitness, Fun & a Pinch of Reality

When I’m not coding or tinkering, you’ll likely find me hitting the gym or enjoying a competitive round of FIFA. I’m fairly spontaneous with plans—though I like to have a rough outline for big goals, I don’t sweat the tiny details of what I’ll be doing a decade from now. I’m a dreamer, sure, but I keep my feet on the ground (even if I sometimes indulge in flights of fancy, courtesy of the games and animations I love).

Looking ahead, I see myself expanding both my personal life and my professional horizons: maybe a big house with a garden (to host epic gatherings of friends), a chance to lead innovative projects, and perhaps a dream yacht for summer adventures. Through it all, I’ll keep building—whether it’s cabinets, software, or relationships—because that’s where I find my purpose.
